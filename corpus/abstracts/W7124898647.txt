Some Japanese dialectologists have proposed classification frameworks of Japanese dialects in the previous century. Some attempted to classify dialects at the national level, and some others attempted to classify them at the subregional level. They generally selected some linguistic features such as lexical alternation, pitch accent, and verb/adjective conjugation, and proposed classification patterns. However, they hardly provided quantitative data with inferential statistical analyses. Therefore, these frameworks have been untested up to now. The purpose of the present study was to investigate how Kansai dialects are classified using random forest and multiple imputation. Our results demonstrated that each prefecture in the area generally has its own dialect, and that a few prefectural dialects spread across administrative divisions. They also demonstrated some crucial words and phrases to classify the dialects. This is the first study providing some supportive and unsupportive evidence for two proposed classification frameworks in Kansai region.
