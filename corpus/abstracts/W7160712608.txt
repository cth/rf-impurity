Untargeted metabolomics studies often identify less than 30% of detected spectral features, with the remaining 70% of features widely referred to as metabolomic “dark matter”. Here we developed a machine-learning pipeline, MS2FinProp, that utilizes the relationship between high-resolution MS/MS data and molecular fingerprints, combined with physicochemical property prediction, to improve the annotation of relevant unknown metabolites. The method uses 166 Molecular ACCess System (MACCS) fingerprints predicted by optimized random forest models trained on MS/MS spectra of annotated compounds. In parallel, key molecular properties, including octanol-water partition coefficient (logP), topological polar surface area (TPSA), and chromatographic retention time (RT), were predicted and used to refine candidate filtering. The predicted fingerprints and properties for uncharacterized spectral features were then queried against the Human Metabolome Database (HMDB) using a hybrid similarity metric that integrates the Tanimoto coefficient (TC) with property-based similarity. The method was evaluated using a high-resolution LC-MS/MS metabolomics dataset derived from human plasma comprising 1071 annotated and 1902 unknown features. Dimensionality reduction on MS/MS spectra preserved 97% of spectral variance within 25 components, and fingerprint prediction achieved a mean accuracy of 0.867 across 3-fold cross validation. Using a TC threshold >0.7, MS2FinProp retrieved at least one HMDB candidate for 1025 of the 1902 unidentified spectra, thereby providing candidate-level assignments for 53.8% of unknown metabolites. The method was further validated on a metabolomics dataset derived from human urine and retrieved 79.3% of reference-standard-confirmed metabolites within the top 100 ranked candidates. These results demonstrate that MS2FinProp can effectively bridge high-resolution MS/MS data to molecular structures, offering a scalable approach to illuminate metabolomic dark matter across biofluids.
