Fragmentation of detailed soil mapping constrains decision-making in many fields; in Slovakia, 1:5 000-scale mapping of soil ecological units (BPEU) extends only to farmland. So, we have used machine learning to create nationwide, high-resolution soil maps employing both the Slovak soil classification and the World Reference Base. The Random Forest algorithm was used to integrate BPEU data with terrain parameters derived from digital elevation models. Innovations include Recursive Feature Elimination to optimise the predictor set, hybrid sampling (whereby 75% of points are located along the median axes of soil polygons and 25% uniformly distributed across the polygon area) at 25 points per square kilometre, and a 25 m buffer at polygon boundaries. The maps attain Kappa values of 0.778–0.792 and an overall accuracy between 0.79 and 0.89, but the reliability of the input data is a limiting factor, particularly near administrative boundaries.
