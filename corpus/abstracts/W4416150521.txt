Theory and research suggest complex mechanisms underlying the relation between Character Strengths and Occupational Well-Being. This study utilized machine learning to explore these relationships, assessing impacts of Character Strength interactions on Occupational Well-Being. Linear regression and decision tree models were tested via cross-nested validation, with both performing similarly in prediction tasks involving PERMA+4 Well-Being in the workplace. Given that decision tree models are specialized for detecting unspecified interaction effects, these results indicate that the relationship between Character Strengths and Occupational Well-Being may not involve complex interactions. Supplementary analysis of regression coefficients highlighted several nuances, however, finding positive and negative relationships between PERMA+4 and Character Strengths. Exploring individual components of PERMA+4 May offer avenues for future researchers to dive deeper into these nuances. This study ultimately suggests that Character Strengths serve as robust predictors of Occupational Well-Being, while laying foundations for future research aimed at exploring nuanced mechanisms underlying their relation.
